üó∫Ô∏è Our Learning Journey

Our goal is to reflect on how our actions impact local and global issues.

  • Review & Objective: We'll quickly look back at our last lesson and talk about what we need to learn today!
  • The Plastic Age: We'll learn how we got to the "Modern Age" (the Plastic Age) and check what you know about it.
  • Everyday Plastic: We will discuss common plastic items like bottles, food cartons, toys, and furniture. Share your examples!
  • Plastic's Problems: We'll find out how plastic hurts the Earth, like polluting our rivers and seas, hurting animals, and filling up landfills. (It takes hundreds of years to break down!)
  • Microplastics: Learn about microplastic‚Äîtiny pieces of plastic that can even contaminate our food and water. [Image of microplastic magnified]
  • Solutions: We'll talk about how people are trying to control plastic: recycling, new laws, making less plastic, and banning single-use items.
  • Research Teams: We will form four groups to research big plastic topics:
    • Plastic Production (how it's made)
    • Microplastic Sources
    • Recycling Challenges
    • Ocean Garbage Islands (Vortex) [Image of ocean plastic vortex]
  • Infographics: Gather your information and create a fun infographic to show everyone how plastic is made, used, and what happens next!
  • Reflection: Who is responsible for plastic waste? The companies that make it? The people who buy it? Or the government? Share and justify your ideas!
  • Plenary: Quick questions and answers to finish the lesson!
