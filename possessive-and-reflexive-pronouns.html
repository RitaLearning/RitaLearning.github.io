What's a Pronoun? ü§î

Pronouns are little words that take the place of a noun. Today, we're going to learn about two special kinds of pronouns: those that show who something belongs to, and those that point back to the person doing the action!

  • Define and identify Possessive Pronouns.
  • Define and identify Reflexive Pronouns.
  • Explain the key difference between these two types of pronouns.
  • Practice using possessive and reflexive pronouns correctly in sentences.

Possessive Pronouns üôã‚Äç‚ôÄÔ∏è

Possessive Pronouns show ownership or possession. They tell us who something belongs to!

"Is this book yours?"

"That jacket is mine!"

"The blue car is theirs."

"Is that umbrella hers?"

The main possessive pronouns are: mine, yours, his, hers, its, ours, theirs.

Fill in the blank: "We found our lost puppy. It is finally ____!"

Reflexive Pronouns üòå

Reflexive Pronouns are used when the subject of a sentence is also the object. It's like the action "reflects" back on the person doing it!

"He taught himself to play guitar."

"I made this cake myself."

"The cat cleaned itself."

"We prepared ourselves for the test."

The main reflexive pronouns are: myself, yourself, himself, herself, itself, ourselves, yourselves, themselves. They all end in -self or -selves.

Complete the sentence: "Be careful not to cut _______ with that knife."
