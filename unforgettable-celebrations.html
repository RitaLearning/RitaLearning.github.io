LESSON 1: DESCRIPTIVE WRITING

Write an essay titled
Unforgettable Celebration!

Unforgettable Celebrations are special family or school events that remain in our memory because they make us happy.

Enquiry Questions

Let's brainstorm before we write:

  • 1 What was your unforgettable celebration?
  • 2 When and where did it take place?

Your Mission

By the end of this lesson, you should be able to:

  • Choose your own topic.
  • Create a simple outline (Introduction, Description, Conclusion).
  • Write an essay about an unforgettable celebration using sensory details.
THE RECIPE

Key Features of a Magazine Article

A good story needs a good skeleton!

1

Catchy Title

Grab the reader's attention instantly!

2

Introduction

Set the scene: Who, What, When, Where?

3

The Body

Describe the event and activities with details.

4

Conclusion

Reflect on feelings and why it was special.

The Secret Ingredients

To make a reader feel like they are at the party, use your 5 senses!

Sight

Colorful balloons, sparkling candles, confetti floating in the air.

Sound

Loud pop music, friends laughing, the "POP" of a balloon.

Smell

Sweet vanilla cake, cheesy pizza, fresh flowers.

Taste

Sugary icing, fizzy lemonade, salty chips.

Touch

Sticky fingers, smooth wrapping paper, warm hugs.

Show, Don't Tell!

Boring...

"The cake was good."

SUPER!

Exciting!

"The creamy chocolate frosting melted on my tongue, tasting like sweet happiness!"

A Birthday to Remember Forever

Last year, my family had one of the most unforgettable celebrations I have ever experienced. It was my grandmother‚Äôs 70th birthday, and everyone in the family came together to make the day special.

The celebration took place at our house. We decorated the living room with colourful balloons, ribbons and bright lights. My siblings and I helped arrange the chairs and set the tables. We were excited because relatives we had not seen in a long time were coming.

When the guests arrived, there was music, laughter and plenty of food. My mother cooked jollof rice, fried chicken and a large pot of soup. There was also a big chocolate cake with ‚ÄúHappy 70th Birthday‚Äù written on it. The food was so delicious that people kept going back for more.

The best part of the celebration was when my grandmother cut her cake. Everyone clapped and sang for her. She smiled happily and thanked us for making her day memorable. After the cake cutting, we played games and took many pictures.

The celebration ended in the evening, but the joy stayed with me. It was unforgettable because I felt close to my family, and I loved seeing my grandmother so happy. I hope we have more celebrations like this in the future.

It's Your Turn to Write!

Step 1: Mind Map

Grab a piece of paper. Draw a circle in the middle with your event name. Draw lines out for:

  • Sights (Colors, Decor)
  • Sounds (Music, Laughter)
  • Food & Tastes
  • Feelings

Step 2: Draft

Write your essay using the outline:

  • Intro: Hook the reader!
  • Body: Describe the fun.
  • Conclusion: How did you feel?

Step 3: Peer Review

Swap with a partner! Check for:

  • Neat handwriting?
  • Capital letters & full stops?
  • Interesting adjectives?
  • Did they use paragraphs?
